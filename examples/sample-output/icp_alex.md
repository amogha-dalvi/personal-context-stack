# Alex's ICP

## Who they are
- Role: Head of Growth or VP Marketing at a digital health company
- Stage: Series A or B — product exists, users are coming in, but churn is visible and becoming a board-level concern
- Environment: 20-80 person company, growth team is 1-3 people, wearing multiple hats, reporting to a CEO or COO who wants "retention fixed"

## What they're dealing with
- Primary pain: 90-day churn is high and the product team says it's not a product problem, but the growth team doesn't know where else to look
- Underlying cause: acquisition messaging sets wrong expectations — patients sign up expecting one thing, experience another, and leave
- What they've tried: better onboarding flows, engagement emails, in-app nudges, NPS surveys — all product-side fixes that haven't moved the number
- What they believe going in: "We need better activation" or "We need to improve the product experience" — they're looking downstream, not upstream

## The trigger moment
- What happens: a board meeting where churn comes up and someone asks "why aren't the retention features working?" — or they lose a key cohort and the CEO asks for a plan
- Where they look: LinkedIn (follow healthtech growth people), peers at other digital health companies, sometimes Google "healthtech retention strategies"
- What they search for: "how to reduce patient churn", "digital health retention", "healthtech onboarding best practices"

## What makes them ideal
- Why they get results: they have enough data to see the pattern once Alex reframes it — they just haven't looked upstream yet
- What they bring: urgency (board pressure), access to both growth and product data, authority to change messaging and positioning
- How you know them when you see them: they describe the problem as "we can't figure out why patients leave after month 3" and have already tried product-side fixes

## The anti-persona
- Who they are: early-stage founders who don't have enough users yet to have a real churn problem — they think they do but their sample size is <100 patients
- What's different: they need product-market fit, not retention optimization. Alex's system assumes the product works — the problem is upstream in GTM, not in whether the product delivers value
- Why it matters: working with them leads to inconclusive results because there's not enough signal to diagnose, and they can't afford the engagement
