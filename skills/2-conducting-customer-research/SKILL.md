# Conducting Customer Research

Goes inside the ICP's head — from first principles, not data pulls. Uses the person's lived experience of serving, selling to, and talking with their audience to map worldview, objections, language, buying triggers, and attention patterns.

This is **Skill 2** — runs after `defining-icp-segmentation`, before positioning.

## When to Use

- After `icp_[name].md` exists in memory
- Content is accurate but doesn't resonate
- Positioning exists but doesn't land with the audience
- Don't understand why they buy, hesitate, or leave

## Core Pattern

This is a **conversation mining the person's experience of their audience**, not a survey or data exercise. Four veins:

1. **Worldview** — what the audience believes before they meet you, what they've tried, what they think the problem is (which may be wrong)
2. **Language & objections** — the exact words they use, what makes them hesitate, what they need to hear before they move
3. **Buying patterns** — how they make decisions, who else is involved, what the timeline looks like, what triggers action vs. browsing
4. **Attention & environment** — where they spend time, what they consume, who they trust, what formats they engage with

Output saved as `research_[name].md` in memory with type `user`.
